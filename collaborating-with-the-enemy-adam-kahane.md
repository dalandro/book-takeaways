## Background - Conventional Collaboration 

### Quote by Rittel and Webber
"The search for scientific bases for confronting problems of social policy is bound to fail because of the nature of these problems... Policy problems cannot be definitively described. Moreover, in a pluralistic society there is nothing like the indisputable public good; there is no objective definition of equity; policies that respond to social problems cannot be meaningfully correct or false; and it makes no sense to talk about "optimal solutions" to these problems... Even worse, there are no solutions in the sense of definitive answers." - Dilemmas in a General Theory 
of Planning - 1973 

### The problems with conventional, constricted collaboration 
  - construction prevents movement 
  - change management assumes control 
  - "There is only one right answer"

### More on "There is only one right answer
It goes against an existential assessment of things. 
A good starting point is the common quote about "Blind men and the elephant"
But things go further than that;
- See Rittell and Webber quote. 
- You can go as far to say (e.g I know that Confucius said it 2.5k years ago) that different cultures across geographies and time have had different intersecting but distinctive sets of values, and you cannot achieve a universal objective truth or set of rules for all, but instead relative truths and rules. 
- There has to be a pragmatic approach.
