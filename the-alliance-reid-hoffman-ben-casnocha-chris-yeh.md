# The Alliance: Managing Talent in the Networked Age
**Authors:** Reid Hoffman, Ben Casnocha, Chris Yeh

---

## Transformational Tours of Duty 
The middle ground between company loyalty and job-hopping.

A strategic tour of duty - intentional agreement between employer and employee that is a mutually-beneficial mission. The employer gets some goodwill-based guarantee of a strategically valuable project or goal that the employee must execute on. The employee gets career growth opportunity through the mission, and is guided by the employer to achieve the mission. 

## Employee Network Intelligence
Another form of some form of mutual benefit between employer and employee. Company alumni networks that companies help foster (but a bit hands-off - let it be employee-motivated). Beneficial to employees - they maintain or strengthen / gain relationships and connections that can help them e.g get jobs. Beneficial to employers - provides that employees are actively encouraged to solve problems or find ideas by interacting with their network, which. company can take advantage of. 

## Takeaways
- We could approach our interactions with our boss or our reports with the mindset of consciously crafting mutually-beneficial plans, projects, activities, interactions. One helps the other grow. One does useful work that the other can utilize. Whatever it is, it is supposed to be good for both parties, as that would encourage its commitment and success. 
- Don't forget that companies could evolve by absorbing knowledge from the outside world - from tech practices to business insights.
